01Setup

We tested it on an RFP we’d already answered.

KeyBank 2026 B2B, routed through Fallon. $300K, Chicago, middle-market C-suite audience. Crain shipped the final proposal against this brief in November 2025.

This test case was chosen to validate the agent’s output against finalized known answers.

02Inputs

We gave the agent both files available for this RFP in SharePoint.

Two documents fed in at the start:

  • The RFP PDF.
  • Fallon’s kick-off email.
03Discovery

It flagged three issues, asked two questions, then drafted the proposal.

Before picking a single product, the agent flagged three gaps in the brief:

  • Flight dates marked “TBD 2026.”
  • RFP due date five months past (Nov 19, 2025).
  • $300K stated flat in the RFP, framed as “recommend a budget” in Fallon’s email.

Then it went further on the flight question. Before asking anything, it did the homework:

  • Named the 2026 CCB events already past as of today: Newsmakers, Women of Influence, Forum Thought Leadership, Power Breakfast #1, Spring Real Estate Breakfast.
  • Named the 11 H2 events still open — C-Suite Conversations, GC Breakfast Brief, Fast 50 Reveal, Power Lunch, Best Places to Work, Elevate, Fall Real Estate Breakfast, Power Breakfast #3, Family Business Lunch, Most Innovative Companies, Future of Food — and flagged which aligned with KeyBank’s middle-market / thought-leadership positioning.

Then it proposed two paths for me to pick between:

aH2 2026 as a fresh push.
bSomething else — partial placement from the original window, 2027 shift, etc.

Plus a separate question on budget: is $300K firm or flexible?

My reply was one letter: a. Budget confirmed as $300K working ceiling. From there the agent pulled pricing from Smartsheet, cross-referenced the toolkit for event tiers, ran constraint checks, and drafted the proposal.

Context-awareness The agent knew the product catalog. Understood today’s date. Flagged that the RFP’s due date was five months past. Surfaced the events still open for H2. Asked clarifying questions and offered context-aware options.

06Handoff

What the agent handed the AE to double-check before send.

Items the agent wasn’t willing to finalize on its own. The handoff from AI to AE.

  1. Event dates TBD. C-Suite Conversations and Family Business Lunch show TBD in the 2026 toolkit. If C-Suite lands before May 7, the 14-day lead is tight. Confirm with the Crain events team this week.
  2. Executive Insights 84-day lead. Earliest launch from today is mid-July 2026. Plan assumes Q3 publication; magazine insert timing drives the schedule.
  3. Politics exclusion vs. CCB editorial. CCB covers Chicago politics and policy as core editorial. Resolve via content-category targeting at the ad-server level. Confirm implementation with ad ops.
  4. Publicis rates. Fallon is Publicis Worldwide. If Crain holds a Publicis MSA, final invoicing may shift. AE to confirm with Crain finance before submission.
  5. 75% MRC viewability. CCB display typically exceeds 75%. MOAT tags accepted. Include explicit guarantee language in the final PPT.
  6. Budget framing. $300K stated flat in the RFP, framed as recommendation in Fallon’s email. Built to $300K ceiling. Mix scales if agency invites a higher recommendation.
  7. Frequency caps not published. CCB pricing sheet carries no per-product caps. Coordinate with ad ops if KeyBank trafficking has specific requirements.
