01Setup

We tested it on an RFP we’d already answered.

03Discovery

It pulled 16 fields, asked four questions, then drafted the proposal.

Before picking a single product, the agent pulled 16 fields from the RFP and Fallon’s email — each tied to the page or line it came from. One field flagged ambiguous:

  • Budget. $300K stated flat in the RFP. “Recommend a budget” in Fallon’s email.

Flight dates came back “TBD 2026” — not actionable for lead-time or date constraint checks. Surfaced as one of the confirmation questions.

The agent asked four questions in one turn before composing:

  • Budget treatment — $300K as working ceiling, or draft against the “recommend” framing?
  • Flight window — full year, a specific quarter, tied to a named CCB event?
  • Geography scope — Chicago-only, or multi-market per the “Expansion Markets” title?
  • Recognition programs — events in scope, or surface the full H2 CCB slate?

My reply was one sentence: 300k ceiling, h2 2026 for date and scoped to chicago. The agent locked context, pulled Smartsheet, and flagged three catalog findings on its way to drafting:

  • Print rate-card anomaly. Row for “Full page ad 2” at $171,542 — inconsistent with neighboring print rates (half page $9,163, quarter page $5,050). Excluded from the mix, flagged for catalog review.
  • No 2026 event-tier pricing in Smartsheet. Agent used the Executive Insights flat-rate row ($40,000) as a Corporate-tier proxy rather than inventing a number. Event line flagged low confidence pending firm 2026 quote from the events team.
  • Audio creative ask unmatched. RFP requests :30s pre-produced audio. CCB Smartsheet surfaces no host-read podcast or programmatic audio inventory. Agent flagged as a brief-back decision rather than forcing a stretch product into the mix.

Then it ran the nine-rule constraint check, composed the mix, and returned a draft.

Context-awareness Pulled 16 fields with per-source citations. Flagged an ambiguous field before asking. Asked four scoped questions in one turn, not drip-by-drip. Caught a data anomaly and excluded it. Named a proxy when the 2026 event-tier card was missing, and surfaced the audio-creative gap rather than guessing a product into the mix.

07Handoff

What the agent handed the AE to double-check before send.

Items the agent wasn’t willing to finalize on its own. The handoff from AI to AE.

  1. Event-tier pricing is a proxy. Crain’s Elevate (Sept) priced at $40,000 using the Executive Insights flat-rate row as a Corporate-tier proxy. No 2026 event-tier card in the catalog yet. Confirm firm 2026 quote with the Crain events team before contract; split into Sponsored Content units if tier pricing differs materially.
  2. 75% MRC viewability and MOAT. CCB display typically exceeds 75%. Confirm with ad ops that the measurement vendor is on Publicis’s accepted list and that MOAT specifically can be accepted.
  3. Audio creative ask unmatched. RFP requests :30s pre-produced audio. CCB Smartsheet surfaces no host-read podcast or programmatic audio inventory. Either remove audio from Fallon’s creative requirements for this campaign, or source via a separate Crain audio partner outside this proposal.
  4. Publicis rates. Fallon is Publicis Worldwide. Smartsheet catalog does not reflect agency discount. Apply contracted Publicis discount at contract stage.
  5. Content exclusions. RFP excludes Kids, Politics, Religious, and Spanish-language inventory. CCB B2B inventory aligns. Resolve Politics overlap via content-category targeting at the ad-server level; confirm implementation with ad ops.
  6. Budget framing. $300K stated flat in the RFP, framed as recommendation in Fallon’s email. Built to $300K ceiling. If Fallon opens a higher recommendation, natural expansion paths: second webcast flight, Presenting-tier event escalation, extended Morning 10 cadence.
  7. Brand-study added value not scoped. RFP welcomes brand-study added value; no specific line in the mix. If Fallon pushes at brief-back, propose a topline brand-lift study attached to the Webcast or Crain’s Elevate sponsorship as a no-cost added-value layer.
  8. Print rate-card anomaly. Smartsheet row for “Full page ad 2” at $171,542 is inconsistent with neighboring print rates (half page $9,163, quarter page $5,050). Row excluded from this mix and flagged for catalog review.

09First pass

First pass against a real RFP. Iterations move quickly.

End-to-end output. Per-line provenance. Constraint checks clean.

Open items land in three lanes:

  • Corpus content — missing or stale catalog entries (2026 event-tier card, per-product toolkit extracts).
  • Prompt tuning — output shape, phrasing, added-value surfacing.
  • Stakeholder decisions — brand voice, library structure, intake process.

Pilot feedback loop with the team closes them in short cycles.
