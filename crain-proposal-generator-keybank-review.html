Slide 06 · What the AI flagged for AE review

Seven items worth a second read before send.

  1. C-Suite Conversations date is TBD. If it lands before May 7, the 14-day booking lead is tight. Confirm with events team immediately.
  2. Executive Insights Roundtable has 84-day lead. Earliest print insert publishes mid-July. Scheduled accordingly.
  3. Politics exclusion vs. CCB editorial. CCB covers Chicago politics as core editorial (Pritzker, mayoral races, pension reform). Resolved via content-category targeting exclusions at ad-server level. Confirm with ad ops.
  4. Publicis rates. Fallon is Publicis Worldwide. If Crain holds a Publicis MSA, final invoicing may differ. AE to confirm with finance.
  5. Viewability guarantee. CCB inventory typically exceeds 75% MRC. MOAT tags accepted. Will state explicitly in the PPT deck.
  6. Budget framing. $300K treated as a working ceiling. Fallon’s email asked for recommendation. Mix scales cleanly if agency returns asking to go higher.
  7. Frequency caps not published. CCB’s sheet carries no per-product caps. Standard best-practice caps will be configured at ad-server level to Fallon’s preference.

Slide 08 · What the AI got right

Five strong signals.

  1. Events priced at real tier rates. C-Suite Conversations Presenting at $40K. Fast 50 Corporate at $25K. Family Business Lunch Corporate at $25K. These match what Andy Rasero put in the real proposal. AI pulled them from the product toolkit extract.
  2. Pillar-based narrative. Budget reads “Events / Thought Leadership / Digital / Always-On” — not a product family dump. Same frame as the real proposal.
  3. Calendar reality checked. RFP was due November 2025. Proposal date is April 2026. Agent caught that March and early-April events already passed and asked about H2 flight before composing. Did not force a Jan 1 start.
  4. Fallon’s kick-off nudge answered. “Last year we ran Content Stories — should we look into this again?” AI proposed a 3× Sponsored Content series at the 3-piece tier rate. Directly addresses the agency’s specific question.
  5. Audience data cited from the media kit. 63% director+, 37% C-suite, 6.5× Bombora multiplier on local business topics, 79% newsletter engagement, 3,400+ annual event attendees. Same stats the real proposal opens with.
Slide 09 · What the AI missed

Three gaps worth closing.

  1. Middle Market Growth Toolkit. $35K flagship lead-gen engine in the real proposal — gated downloadable resource built by Crain’s Content Studio, purpose-shaped for KeyBank’s mid-market audience. Named in the product toolkit. Agent didn’t pick it. Either swap into the mix in place of one Thought Leader Report, or add if budget accommodates.
  2. Interstitial and App Takeover. Real proposal includes both as always-on digital. AI chose Audience Targeting Display instead. Similar shape, different units. Judgment call — worth asking whether a real AE would actually pick Interstitial over Audience Targeting for KeyBank.
  3. Tiered added-value framing. Real proposal structures Brand Perception Module as added value at $125K+ and Brand Lift Study as added value at $200K+. AI mentions brand study as an option but doesn’t tier it. Small polish; real AE would structure it as a differentiator.
Slide 10 · What this means for production

In production, this is native.

  • Files read automatically. Enterprise Claude reaches the media kit, product toolkit, and prior proposals through the hosted Microsoft 365 connector. The same files we attached manually for this test load at turn zero.
  • Output is a PowerPoint. Enterprise Claude renders the deliverable as a .pptx shaped like Crain’s standard decks. This webpage is the test artifact, not the production render.
  • AE refines conversationally. “Drop Family Business Lunch, add Power Breakfast.” “Swap Audience Targeting for Interstitial.” “Add Middle Market Toolkit.” Minutes, not restarts.
  • AI is the starting point. AE is still the author.
