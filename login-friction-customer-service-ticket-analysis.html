02Identity mismatch

17% of password reset failures are users filing from the wrong email.

  • 22% file from an email not on the Pelcro account. 33 of 138 resolvable (150-ticket sample, 13 brands).
  • 76% of those have a different email on the same Pelcro account. 25 of 33, by account_number lookup.
  • Same-domain (different local-part, same employer): 45%. j.smith@firm.com filing vs jsmith@firm.com on file.
  • Different-domain (personal vs work, or job change): 30%. Common at P&I (job turnover) and AN (personal vs work).
  • Typo, Tire Business #290241: filing ***@discounttires.com, on file ***@discounttire.com. The typo’d address has been billed and renewed.
  • Reset / magic link goes to the canonical email on file, not the email the user is filing from. The 17% recurs with any email-based credential.

Source: 150-ticket sample of password-reset-failure tickets, last 180 days. Zendesk resolution + Sailthru lookup by email then by extid. 22% is a lower bound; per-brand n is small. See A4.

03Two findings

Half of password reset filers are reading email near time of ticket submission. “Unresolvable” likely a different issue.

  • 50 of 150 (33%): real-email open within ±14 days of ticket, no real-click.
  • 25 of 150 (17%): real-click within ±14 days.
  • Median time from ticket to user’s click: 0.8h — the customer service agent-rescue pattern.
  • Email is reaching the engaged half. The friction is after the email.
  • Three possible downstream issues the data can’t distinguish: link expired or rendered poorly; sign-in didn’t complete; reset arrived slower than expected.
  • Magic links remove the password but use the same email channel and sign-in flow.

Bucket 1 · Structural patterns

A1Plastics News

Plastics News’ 16.5% login share is a CSR-template artifact, not per-capita friction.

  • PN leads the portfolio in login share (16.5% vs 12.6% portfolio average). The gap is structural, not a per-capita defect.
  • Welcome@1 stock-reset language appears in 42% of PN login tickets vs 31% portfolio. CSR macro mechanically tags any access call as a password reset.
  • PN’s institutional-access mention rate is the lowest in the portfolio: 2.8% (IP whitelist, VPN, firewall, proxy) vs 8.1% portfolio.
  • PN resolves faster than portfolio, not slower. Time-to-first-solve under 1h: 70.5% PN vs 66.2% portfolio. 30-day same-requester repeat rate: 12.9% PN vs 13.5% portfolio.
  • Magic links would address ~50% of PN login tickets (password-reset + reset-link-missing). To realize the deflection, the CSR Welcome@1 macro will need updating in parallel or templated-reset behavior will continue to generate password-reset-tagged volume.
Supports
  • PN tag rate, repeat rate, and resolve speed all align with CSR-workflow explanation.
  • Lowest IP/firewall rate rules out the institutional-access framing the research deck slide-10 quote suggests.
Does not support
  • Pelcro/Redshift cut by login tickets per 1K active PN subscribers not pulled. Would settle the per-capita question definitively.

Source: 24-month corpus. Scripts pn_deepdive.py, pn_tag_check.py.

A3Welcome cohort

8% of the welcome cohort sits in Sailthru hardbounce engagement.

  • 601 of 13,053 login tickets (4.6%) carry an agent-applied Welcome@1 / Welcome1 temp password. Call-channel cases by definition — the agent set the password during a live CS interaction.
  • Top brands: Auto News, Crain’s Detroit, Modern Healthcare. PN over-indexes (42% of PN login tickets contain the language vs 31% portfolio) per A1.
  • Welcome-cohort deliverability is degraded: 8% of welcome-cohort tickets are from users in hardbounce engagement at Sailthru vs ~3% baseline at AA, P&I, MH, AN. A post-call magic-link email travels the same channel as the original reset email. For ~1 in 12 of the welcome cohort, the channel itself is broken.
  • The deflection target is upstream. Magic-link entry on the paywall converts the pre-call population — users who hit the paywall, never call CS, and either resolve the access themselves or churn silently.

Source: corpus regex \bWelcome[@]?1\b over ticket subject + description + comments. Welcome-cohort engagement profile from cohort_contrast_welcome_with_sailthru.json.

Bucket 2 · Methodology

A4Identity mismatch

Identity-mismatch methodology and what the data does and does not support.

  • Cohort: all reset_link_missing tickets last 180 days, B2B admin filers excluded, end-user language confirmed. N=601 in corpus; 150-ticket random sample drawn with seed 42.
  • Email resolution: Zendesk /api/v2/users/show_many for all 150 requester_ids.
  • In-Sailthru lookup: brand-mapped Sailthru /user GET with key=email.
  • F-bucket identity-mismatch test: for tickets returning user-not-found by email, a second Sailthru /user GET with key=extid and the Zendesk account_number. Canonical Sailthru email then compared to the Zendesk filing email.
Supports
  • A 22% rate of email-mismatch on filed password-reset-failure tickets.
  • 76% of those resolve to a confirmed different email at the same Pelcro account.
  • Patterns consistent with same-domain alias differences, job change, and documented typos.
Does not support
  • Generalizing 22% to all subscribers — survey-takers and ticket-filers self-select; this measures the ticket population only.
  • Generalizing the same-domain vs different-domain breakdown across brands — per-brand n is small at most brands.
  • Distinguishing identity mismatch from duplicate subscription on the same email family — the two patterns are not mutually exclusive at the account-record level.

Source: scripts extract_cohorts.py, zd_resolve.py, sailthru_lookup.py, classify_dispositions.py, identity_mismatch.py in sailthru-cross-reference/.

A5ARC pre/post

ARC pre/post methodology and confounds.

  • ARC dates source: Aha PROD-S-5 (ARC Migration) initiative and child epics. Used actual_done_at where available; fell back to due_date. Auto News + autonewsca date (10/24/2025) provided directly.
  • Window: per brand, 90 days before actual_done_at and 90 days after, capped at corpus start (05/15/2024) and corpus end (05/15/2026).
  • Login-subtype counts: is_login_related per corpus signal layers (tag, sub-category, text). Per-day rate = count / window days.
  • Volume control: login share = login tickets / total tickets in the same window (after B2B admin exclusion).
Supports
  • Brand-by-brand pre/post comparison of operational ticket rates at known ARC dates.
  • Decline at AA, P&I, MH, AN consistent across reset-link-missing, password-reset, and re-login subtypes at Ad Age (most complete post-window).
  • City Brands re-login spike concentrated in engaged users, not dormant — rules out the simplest “already-churned users” alternative.
Does not support
  • Causal attribution to ARC specifically. ARC migrations are correlated with concurrent changes (Sophi rollout per brand, Piano integration, template revisions, sender reputation, signup-flow updates).
  • Distinguishing the three plausible technical root causes for the City Brands spike — cookie-domain mismatch, TTL regression, identity-sync race — without Pelcro telemetry.
  • Calendar-pure comparison. The 90-day window crosses calendar boundaries; seasonal effects sit underneath the comparison.

Source: scripts fetch_arc_dates.py, arc_prepost.py, arc_robustness.py, re-login_cohorts.py, re-login_classify.py in sailthru-cross-reference/.

A6§03 methodology

Password-reset-failure cohort engagement classification and repeat-chain decomposition.

  • Engagement classification (Panel A): per ticket, looked up requester email in brand-mapped Sailthru. D = real-open within ±14 days of ticket, no real-click. E = real-click within ±14 days. Universe = 150-ticket primary cohort (same as §02).
  • Classification limit: Sailthru’s public API does not expose per-send event history. D and E use the user’s most recent real-open and real-click timestamps across all sends, not specifically the reset send.
  • Repeat-chain decomposition (Panel B): 24-month corpus iteration over requester_id chains. Window = 30 days. Pair-type classification on consecutive login-ticket pairs (sticky = same subtype, evolving = different subtype, multi-brand = different brand). B2B admin filers excluded.
  • 1,342 repeater chains across 10,157 unique non-admin login-ticket-filers (13.2% repeat rate, matches the deck’s 14% unresolvable within a percentage point).
Supports
  • Panel A: a population estimate that ~50% of password-reset-failure tickets are filed by users demonstrably engaged with the brand’s email stream near the ticket date.
  • Panel A: the agent-rescue pattern (median time from ticket creation to user real-click event = 0.8 hours).
  • Panel B: a 54% evolving share that is structurally different from the 42% sticky share — different transitions on the second ticket.
  • Panel B: convergence between corpus repeat-within-30d (13.2%) and survey unresolvable rate (14%) at the headline level.
Does not support
  • Panel A: distinguishing among the three plausible downstream patterns (link expiry, post-click auth failure, impatience). Sailthru data tops out at email engagement.
  • Panel A: the D-class population is an upper bound on inbox-reachability and a lower bound on email-channel friction (users could be opening newsletters fine while the reset email specifically failed).
  • Panel B: counting users who give up silently — bounded to ticket-side data.
  • Panel B: the 34% <1h sub-bucket is mostly CSR ticket-splitting (one inbound call generates two ticket records), not real user re-contact. The true “fix didn’t stick within a day” rate is closer to ~10%.
  • Panel B: sticky vs evolving depends on the corpus’s login-subtype regex. Tickets where the regex forces one subtype despite the body containing multiple problems may be misclassified.

Source: 150-ticket primary cohort + 24-month corpus repeat-chain iteration. Scripts classify_dispositions.py, unresolvable_decompose.py in sailthru-cross-reference/. Output disposition-detail.csv, unresolvable-decomp.json.

Bucket 3 · Scope

A8Out of scope

What this work does not and cannot address.

  • Per-user per-send Sailthru events. Sailthru’s public API does not expose them. Closing this gap would require warehouse-side ingest of Sailthru’s data feed. Material for distinguishing the three downstream patterns in §03 Panel A (link expiry vs post-click auth vs impatience).
  • Hart Energy. Has its own ARC date (09/19/2025) and login-friction profile but no Sailthru-brand mapping in this account. Pre/post ticket-side analysis was run; Sailthru cross-reference is not possible without identifying the actual sender.
  • Newsletter delivery failures. Different email class, different rate-limit pool, different reputation. Out of scope.
  • B2B admin clearinghouse workflow. Williams Lea, Couranto, Prenax, WT Cox, EBSCO, internal Crain Sales Ops batch-filers excluded from all user-impact metrics. Separate operational investigation.
  • Per-1K active-subscriber normalization via Pelcro/Redshift. Not pulled. Would settle PN’s per-capita question (A1) and tighten per-brand comparisons in §01.
  • Adobe Analytics paywall events. Would resolve the post-click auth-flow question in §03 Panel A. Sailthru data tops out at email engagement.
  • Sender-domain reputation (link.adage.com and equivalents). Could explain residual brand variance in bounce rate. External tools required.
