Crain Answers — State Across Five Brands

Performance overview, what subscribers use it for, and what’s next.

00Snapshot

What the data shows about adoption, quality, and what’s next.

  • Quality is high. The AI answers accurately when the content exists.
  • The gap is data. Subscriber demand clearly points to sales numbers, rankings, and financial tables — the AI doesn’t have access to them.
  • Half of subscribers don’t return after their first query. This pattern is consistent across all brands.
  • Adoption peaks in week 2 and settles by week 5 on every brand. The steady level varies 6–10x. AutoNews leads.
  • Two in-flight initiatives address the biggest gaps. New on-site components create contextual entry points to Crain Answers. The Crain IQ initiative gives the AI access to structured rankings and financial data.

Snapshot as of 05/15/2026. Five brands live, 23–100 days each.

02Quality and the gap

Quality is high. The gap is data, not hallucinations.

  • Hallucinations: 1.0–3.5% per brand. Across 2,100 quality-checked responses.
  • Completeness: 4.32–4.59 of 5.
  • Relevance: 4.82–4.89 of 5.
  • Subscriber feedback is 2-to-1 positive when they rate. Negative feedback is unanimous across brands — subscribers want sales tables, ranked lists, and financial figures the AI can’t pull from articles.
  • Same data gap repeats brand by brand. Sales by nameplate (AutoNews). Health-system margins (MH). Cap rates and zoning (CCB). Multi-year archives (CDB). Ad-spend benchmarks (Ad Age).
Plan to address · Crain IQ

AI will answer from data: sales tables, rankings, financial figures. Pilot at Pensions & Investments. AutoNews next. See A5.

03The funnel leak

Roughly half of subscribers do not convert to habit.

One-and-done holds across every brand

Excludes internal/test user IDs. Source: Crain Answers analytics database, pulled 05/15/2026.
  • 44–63% of subscribers query once and never return. Pattern holds on every brand.
  • AutoNews retains best (44% one-and-done, 15 heavy users). CCB worst (63%, zero heavy users).
  • Suggested prompts dominate first questions. Top 1–4 queries on every brand are suggested prompts. When subscribers ask their own questions, no single topic gets much volume.
  • Quick win: routinely refresh suggested prompts per brand. Counters the click-through decay we see on launch prompts.
Plan to address · on-site components

Contextually relevant entry points on the homepage, on article pages, and a permanent site-nav link replace the promotional banner. Ad Age public launch 06/04, then the other four brands in sequence.

09Conclusion

Crain Answers is mid-transformation with clear traction signals. The biggest gaps have solutions in flight.

  • Traction is real. Strong answer quality across all brands (1–3.5% hallucination; completeness 4.32–4.59 of 5). AutoNews sustains 30–50 weekly active subscribers. Ad Age has built a sustained-research audience.
  • The biggest gaps are clear and addressable. Subscribers want data the AI can’t currently reach (sales tables, rankings, financials). Half of subscribers don’t return after their first query.
  • Crain IQ closes the data gap. Gives the AI access to structured rankings and financial data. Pilot at Pensions & Investments. AutoNews next.
  • On-site components close the adoption gap. Contextually relevant entry points on the homepage, on article pages, and a permanent site-nav link replace the promotional banner. Ad Age launches 06/04. Sequential rollout follows.
  • Smaller moves queued behind. Refresh suggested prompts monthly. Re-engagement emails 6–8 weeks post-launch. Auto-respond to “is this free?” questions. Weekly thumbs-down review.
