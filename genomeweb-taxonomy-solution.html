GenomeWeb moves to Arc on a closed tag vocabulary. In service of sponsors and readers.

01The direction

Arc maintains a closed tag vocabulary. Drupal long-tail tags drop at cutover.

  • Closed tag vocabulary in Arc. Story tags governed. New tags governed (process to be defined).
  • Drupal long tail drops at cutover. 86% of Drupal remnants do not migrate.
  • Sponsor and reader commitments preserved. What changes is structure and process.
Where we land A focused vocabulary migrates to Arc. The long tail retires at cutover. A subset remains flagged for editorial review — room to prune further in the 12- and 24-month usage tabs.

Sources: GNW taxonomy decision doc (2026-04-21); six-year Drupal content audit (~2,100 tags, ~300 migrate, ~1,800 drop, 61 gray). Pulled 04/2026.

02Sponsor

Sponsor campaigns stay the same. Sailthru’s backend goes thematic.

Same pitch to sponsors. Same editorial. Same exclusivity per send.

Current
  • Sponsor picks tags. Gets exclusive sponsorship per send. Campaign built bespoke each time.
  • Audience, feed, and template rebuilt per sponsor. No shared infrastructure.
  • Competitor exclusions hand-typed. Up to 90 edits per launch.
Future
  • Sponsor picks tags. Gets exclusive sponsorship per send. Campaign assembled from thematic infrastructure.
  • Standing cohort and feed per theme, reused across sponsors. Each send is a light configuration.
  • Inclusion-tag targeting. Target sponsors in, not competitors out.
Open — ad ops Inclusion-tag targeting is the drawn approach in place of hand-typed competitor exclusions. Feasibility pending with ad ops.

Sources: marketing automation audit; GAM line-item exclusion patterns; sponsor campaign register. Pulled 04/2026. Universe: 15 active GenomeWeb sponsor campaigns.

03Reader

Readers follow sections. Tags power advertising and newsletter infrastructure underneath.

Current
  • Three follow surfaces. Preferences UI, topic page, and article page.
  • Follow and opt-in are separate actions. Require both for the daily newsletter.
  • Full Drupal tag list in My Topics. Companies, drugs, and concepts mixed in.
Future
  • One follow surface. Preferences UI. Topic and article buttons retire.
  • Follow and opt-in in a single action. Following a section opts the user into the newsletter.
  • Curated section list in My Topics. Section surface limited for a better user experience.
Section surface Arc holds sections across the site structure. Editorial curates a focused subset for the My Topics display — not every section surfaces to the reader.

Sources: Sailthru user records; preferences-UI analytics; Drupal taxonomy export; Arc site-structure workbook (211 slots / 207 unique URLs). Pulled 04/2026.
