Adobe instrumentation: Checkout v3 and Server-Side Paywall.

Spec walkthrough for analytics and engineering build. What changes in Adobe Launch and slot mapping. What stays countable in Workspace and Redshift.

00Summary

Proposed checkout and paywall analytics changes — and why.

  • Checkout and paywall rebuild. Checkout (the subscription flow) and the paywall both being rebuilt. This deck proposes how each will report into Adobe Analytics.
  • Current state - every analytics change today is multi-team coordination. Adobe Launch work, slot allocation, Workspace updates — each change needs all three teams to act in lockstep.
  • Proposted state - Named events describe themselves and ship with the code. checkout_started, checkout_step_viewed, checkout_completed — each names what it measures. Steps and payment methods sit in the payload. New fields ship with the build, queryable the day they go live. (Paywall stays on existing page-article + click-hardwall rules with one new eVar mint — paywall.version — to track what paywall was rendered; no new events needed; see §02.)

Spec: derivatives/2026-05-17-checkout-paywall-analytics-contract.md. Cutover: CCB July 30, all brands September 30.

01Recommendation

Named events per checkout phase. Lift-and-shift the paywall.

Checkout v3 instruments to the 2026 industry-standard shape. Server-side paywall replaces Piano at functional parity.

  • Checkout today: 4 Launch rules, one per step. page-sub3 fires on account (event13), page-sub4 on address (event14, V1 only), page-sub5 on payment (event15), page-sub6 on completion (event8). V1/V2 step-order divergence.
  • Checkout v3 shape: 4 named events (checkout_started, checkout_step_viewed, checkout_step_completed, checkout_completed). One Launch rule each. step_name in the payload.
  • checkout_started fires on page-load. step_name enum includes 'plan_selection' as step 1 for browse-mode entry, or 'payment' as step 1 for pre-resolved-plan entry. First step_viewed after checkout_started indicates entry mode.
  • Aligns with modern best practice. Industry-standard shape: named events per funnel phase with step semantics in the payload, one Launch rule per phase. Crain's current checkout is 2015-era Adobe by comparison: per-step rules, numbered event slots.
  • Paywall today: Sophi decision block on every article hit (eVar140–149, 10 slots), two hardwall click writers feeding event4 (hardwall click counter), wall.suppressReason field with no consumer.
  • Paywall MVP shape: no new client events; existing event slots (event86 / event87 / event83) preserved via SSP-direct writes. One new eVar mint: eVar153 ← paywall.version — string identifier of which deployed paywall artifact was shown (e.g., 'hardwall-premium-2026-q3'). Today there's zero historical record of which paywall design/offer was rendered at any given moment; this fixes that. Click flow stays on existing click-hardwall rule; paywall.version inherits via Adobe visit-scoped eVar persistence. Dismiss tracking, variant, experiment, A/B fields land in Phase 2.
  • Sophi removal is forced. Piano contract ends Sept 30. Sophi sunset per brand, July 30 through Sept 30, regardless of analytics-contract choice.
Tradeoff Three per-step counters retire (account, address, payment). Per-step counts move into a step-name breakdown on a single named event. Conversion rate stays comparable to today's measure.

03Continuity

What stays countable. What changes in Workspace. What dbt picks up.

Adobe Workspace counts numeric slots, not Launch rule names. Cutover does not break weekly KPI reads at the slot level.

What Adobe Workspace sees

  • event8 (subscription completion counter) stays event8 regardless of which Launch rule writes to it.
  • Three writers after cutover: checkout-completed (new v3 completion rule), page-sub6 (legacy V1/V2 completion rule, alive on unmigrated brands during rollout), page-upsell3 (upsell completion, out of scope, unchanged).
  • May 2026 row and December 2026 row are directly comparable. No bridge query. No merge model.
# Per-brand weekly subscription completions across the cutover Freeform table: Metric → event8 Dimension → report_suite (brand) Breakdown → week Window → 2026-05-01 through 2026-12-31 # For per-surface attribution after cutover, add prop62 as a secondary breakdown: + prop62 filter IN ('page-sub6', 'checkout-v3-complete', 'paywall-walletpay')

Three edits to historic reports

  • Saved reports filtering on prop62 = 'page-sub6': broaden to IN ('page-sub6', 'checkout-v3-complete', 'paywall-walletpay'). One Workspace edit per report.
  • Per-step counters retire on full v3 rollout: event13 (account), event14 (address), event15 (payment). Per-step analysis becomes a step_name breakdown on the new step-viewed event.
  • New MVP conversion-rate KPI. checkout_started fires on /subscribe page-load. The MVP KPI is checkout_completed / checkout_started. Denominator is everyone who loaded /subscribe, not just users who reached the account form. Not directly comparable to V2's event8 / event13 ratio.
  • V2-comparable funnel is derivable. Filter step_name IN ('payment', 'account', 'address', 'intent_capture') on checkout_step_viewed to strip out 'plan_selection' entrants. The resulting conversion rate matches V2's event13-based denominator and is back-comparable across the cutover.

What Redshift sees

  • No KPI presentation breaks. event8 keeps materializing in cdw_02_stg.digital_events exactly as today. pelcro_daily_subscription_statuses.is_new is server-side, oblivious to the front-end shape. pelcro_weekly_summary and article_performance keep counting completions correctly. Zero dbt model change.
  • Expose prop62 in the curated view (open follow-up). prop62 is the Launch-rule attribution literal. After cutover its literals distinguish v3 completion ('checkout-v3-complete'), paywall wallet-pay ('paywall-walletpay'), and legacy V1/V2 completion ('page-sub6'). Surfacing the column enables surface-attribution analysis: which surface produced which completion. Becomes a KPI, not a convenience field.
  • Add a checkout_version column to the digital_events staging model. Values 'v1' / 'v2' / 'v3', derived from page.name and prop62 literals. Makes dual-publish-window analysis a GROUP BY instead of heuristic re-derivation. Stays useful after rollout for historical cohort splits.
  • Deprecate cdw_03_prs.sophi.sophi_all_brands_daily after Sept 30. Model goes per-brand stale through cutover window. Keep alive for control-cohort analysis until last brand cuts over. Deprecate after, historicals preserved.
  • Phase 2 only: when dynamic paywall decisioning ships, revisit a paywall_decision_id staging column to join checkout-side hits to a richer server decision log. Not in scope for MVP.
